Places

Ladakh

Ladakh is a region administered by India as a union territory, and constitutes a part of the larger region,has been the subject of dispute between China.The eastern end, consisting of the uninhabited Aksai Chin plains, is claimed by the Indian Govt as part of Ladakh.
Mumbai

Mumbai is the capital city of Maharashtra.It is home to three UNESCO World Heritage Sites: the Elephanta Caves, Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus, and the city's distinctive ensemble of Victorian and Art Deco buildings designed in the 19th and 20th centuries.
Rajasthan

Rajasthan covers 342,239 square kilometres of total geographical area.It is on northwestern side, where it comprises most of the wide and inhospitable Thar Desert and shares a border with the Pakistani provinces of Punjab to the northwest and Sindh to the west.
Andaman

Andaman and Nicobar Islands is a union territory of India consisting of 572 islands, of which 38 are inhabited, at the junction of the Bay of Bengal and the Andaman Sea. The island chains are thought to be a submerged extension of the Arakan Mountains.
Kerala

Kerala is the twenty-first largest Indian state by area.Kerala is the thirteenth-largest state by population.With the capital being Thiruvananthapuram. Malayalam is the most widely spoken language and is also the official language of the state.
Sikkim

Sikkim is a state in northeastern India. It borders the Tibet Region of China in the north and northeast,Bhutan in the east , Nepal in the west, and West Bengal in the south.It accounts for the largest share of cardamom production in India.
